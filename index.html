About

The person behind the plan.

Leaving the Air Force was harder than I expected. Not the work part — the people part.

Seven years in Security Forces meant being surrounded by people from every background imaginable — and learning how to work with all of them under pressure. When that ended, the transition to corporate life was quieter than I was ready for. More isolated. I knew how to execute, but I didn't yet know where I fit.

Implementation PM work changed that. It gave me back what I missed most — real problems, real people, and the kind of collaboration where your ability to read a room and build trust actually determines the outcome. Every client is different. Every implementation has a moment where the plan meets reality and you have to think fast. That's where I thrive.

Outside of work I'm in the middle of my first running journey — several 5Ks and 10Ks in, with a half marathon on the calendar before the year is out. I hold an MBA with a marketing concentration and am actively working toward my PMP.

Process

How I work.

01
Kickoff
Structured intake. Documented scope, constraints, and success criteria before a single task begins.
02
Scope Creep
Name it, price it, decide it. Every change is logged, assessed, and brought to a stakeholder decision.
03
Risk
Flagged early with likelihood, impact, and mitigation — not just a RAG status nobody reads.
04
Communication
Right message, right audience. Executives get decisions. Technical teams get specifics.
05
UAT
Formal phase, not an afterthought. Test plans tied to requirements. Sign-off documented, not verbal.
06
Handoff
Clean close. Full documentation. The team inheriting the account shouldn't need to re-ask my questions.
